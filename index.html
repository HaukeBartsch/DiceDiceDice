2 x 0 / (0 + 0) = 0

Models for image segmentation learn by example. A target mask (dark tiles) comes from a human expert.

A deep neural network learns by improving the overlap of its own estimated mask with the human generated mask.

At each learning step the overlap is improved (dice score). A dice score of 1 means a perfect match, at 0 we have a no overlap.

  • How do errors depend on the size of a region?
  • How does the overlap work with more than one region?
  • Is there more than one way to get an 'ok' dice score of 0.86?
